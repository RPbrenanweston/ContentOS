## Executive Summary

This analysis identifies 14 UK grant programmes and funding mechanisms relevant to Exprs and/or Brenan Weston, tiered by immediacy and strategic fit. The fundamental distinction between the two businesses -- Exprs as a product/SaaS innovator and Brenan Weston as a service/consultancy -- significantly shapes eligibility. Product businesses with demonstrable R&D (like Exprs) qualify for a broader range of innovation grants. Consultancy businesses (like Brenan Weston) face tighter eligibility but have strong angles through cybersecurity skills, DASA, and regional programmes.

**Key Findings:**
- Exprs has strong eligibility for 10 of 14 identified programmes
- Brenan Weston has strong eligibility for 5 programmes and partial eligibility for 3 more
- Two programmes are open NOW with deadlines in March 2026
- The AI Assurance Innovation Fund (Spring 2026) is a high-value strategic opportunity for both
- R&D Tax Credits are applicable to both businesses but in different ways

## Grant Landscape Overview

### What Changed in 2025-2026
- Smart Grants PAUSED from January 2025. No Smart rounds in FY 2025/26. Replaced by Growth Catalyst: New Innovators
- DASA Open Call CLOSED from January 2026. New service reopens under UKDI in July 2026
- Record UK R&D budget: GBP 86 billion across the spending review period (FY 2026/27 to 2029/30), with UKRI allocated GBP 38.6 billion
- AI Action Plan driving new grant streams: AI Assurance Innovation Fund (GBP 11 million), AI Growth Zones, Sovereign AI series

### Service vs Product Distinction
Most Innovate UK grants explicitly favour product/technology innovation (TRL progression, prototyping, IP creation). Consultancy businesses can qualify when they:
- Develop proprietary tooling or methodology (not just deliver services)
- Contribute to collaborative R&D projects as a partner
- Focus on skills, ecosystem, or sector development
- Address defence/security challenges (DASA is more flexible on business model)

## R&D Tax Credits Analysis

### HMRC R&D Tax Relief

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| **Administering Body** | HMRC |
| **Benefit** | Enhanced tax deduction on qualifying R&D expenditure |
| **Status** | Always available (claim on annual tax return) |
| **Key Change 2025** | Cloud computing, AI, and data-driven project costs now included |
| **Application URL** | https://www.gov.uk/guidance/corporation-tax-research-and-development-rd-relief |
| **Complexity** | Medium (recommend specialist adviser) |

### Exprs -- R&D Tax Credits: STRONG CASE

Exprs has a strong R&D tax credit claim because:
- Developing AI-driven candidate matching algorithms involves technological uncertainty (qualifying criterion)
- SaaS platform architecture, scalability challenges, and novel AI features qualify as R&D
- Cloud computing costs for AI training/inference now eligible (from April 2025)
- Software development that pushes beyond standard industry solutions qualifies
- First-time claimants must submit claim notification within 6 months of accounting year end

**Estimated Qualifying Activities:**
- AI model development and training for recruitment matching
- Novel workflow automation algorithms
- Platform scalability R&D
- Data processing and ML pipeline innovation
- Security and privacy engineering for candidate data

### Brenan Weston -- R&D Tax Credits: MODERATE CASE

Brenan Weston can claim R&D tax credits IF:
- Developing bespoke AI tooling for cyber talent matching (not just using off-the-shelf tools)
- Building proprietary methodology that resolves technological uncertainty
- Creating novel approaches to security clearance verification or skills assessment
- The R&D must be in the technology, not the consultancy methodology

**Key Distinction:** Using ChatGPT or existing AI tools to support consultancy work does NOT qualify. Building custom AI systems that advance the state of the art DOES qualify.

**Recommendation:** Engage an R&D tax credit specialist to audit qualifying activities. Even a moderate claim on GBP 100k of qualifying spend could yield significant tax benefit.

## Strategic Recommendations

### For Exprs

**Immediate (February-March 2026):**
- Innovation Loans Round 25 is the highest-impact opportunity available right now. GBP 100k-5m loan for late-stage R&D and commercialisation maps directly to an AI SaaS platform at TRL 7-8. Deadline is 4 March 2026.

**Short-term (Spring 2026):**
- AI Assurance Innovation Fund should be a priority application. Position Exprs as building AI assurance mechanisms for recruitment AI -- this aligns with EU AI Act compliance (employment is classified as high-risk AI use) and UK regulatory direction.

**Medium-term (H2 2026):**
- Watch for next Growth Catalyst round and new Smart Grant format when announced
- Consider Collaborative AI Solutions if partnerships with large enterprises and universities can be established (construction or transport sector recruitment angle)

**Strategic Positioning:**
- Frame all grant applications around AI innovation, not recruitment
- Emphasise: algorithmic fairness, responsible AI in hiring, workforce productivity through automation
- TRL 7-8 positions Exprs for commercialisation-focused grants, not early-stage R&D
- Innovation Loans suit the scale and stage better than small grants

### For Brenan Weston

**Immediate (February-March 2026):**
- Begin preparation for AI Assurance Innovation Fund (Spring 2026). This is the single best strategic opportunity. The intersection of AI security, assurance, and workforce is precisely what this fund targets.
- If Brenan has any proprietary AI tooling under development, consider Innovation Loans Round 25 (deadline 4 March).

**Short-term (H1 2026):**
- Engage with NCSC and NPSA networks ahead of the next Cyber Local cycle. Building relationships with local steering groups now positions Brenan for strong applications later.

**Medium-term (H2 2026):**
- DASA/UKDI Open Call (July 2026) is a natural fit. Frame the application around AI-enabled identification and rapid deployment of cyber talent for defence and national security.
- Consider AISI grants if they reopen -- moderate fit but worth attempting.

**Strategic Positioning:**
- The consultancy model is a limitation for most innovation grants. Mitigate this by:
  1. Developing identifiable proprietary AI tools (even internal ones) that can be described as product innovation
  2. Positioning any bespoke methodology as IP that resolves technological uncertainty
  3. Leading collaborative bids where Brenan contributes domain expertise alongside a tech partner
- The cybersecurity skills gap narrative is powerful -- use it in every application
- DASA/UKDI is the most consultancy-friendly funding body -- prioritise this relationship

### Second-Order Strategic Insights

1. **Both businesses should document R&D activities NOW.** Even if a formal R&D tax credit claim is months away, contemporaneous records of technological challenges, experiments, and solutions are essential for HMRC compliance and dramatically strengthen claims.

2. **The AI Assurance Innovation Fund is the single most strategic grant for both businesses.** It is new (Spring 2026), well-funded (GBP 11 million), and sits at the intersection of AI regulation, responsible deployment, and sector-specific assurance -- exactly where both Exprs (recruitment AI assurance) and Brenan (security AI assurance) can credibly position.

3. **Grant applications build institutional capability.** Even unsuccessful applications force articulation of innovation strategy, R&D roadmaps, and commercial plans. These artefacts are reusable across all future applications and investor conversations.

4. **The UK government is spending at record levels on AI.** GBP 86 billion in R&D across the spending review, AI Growth Zones, Sovereign AI investment, and the AI Action Plan mean that 2026-2028 will see more AI grant opportunities than any previous period. Building grant application muscle now pays compounding returns.
