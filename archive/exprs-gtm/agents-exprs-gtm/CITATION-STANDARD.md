# CITATION-STANDARD.md

**Purpose:** Defines the Harvard Referencing Format standard for all Exprs GTM deliverables -- CMO whitepapers, CRO sales collateral, blog content, email sequences, and any future agent outputs.

**Effective Date:** 13 February 2026
**Applies To:** All CMO and CRO agents, content contributors, and copywriters

---

## 1. Why Harvard Format

Harvard referencing is used across all Exprs deliverables for three reasons:

1. **Credibility:** Third-party citations establish Exprs positioning as data-informed, not opinion-driven.
2. **Verifiability:** Every numerical claim can be traced to its source by any reader, reviewer, or compliance auditor.
3. **Consistency:** A single citation format across whitepapers, emails, call scripts, and handoff documents prevents style drift and reduces editing overhead.

---

## 2. In-Text Citation Format

### Standard Rule

Every factual claim, data point, or statistic must include an in-text citation immediately following the claim.

**Format:** (Author, Year)

### Examples

| Claim | Correct Citation |
|-------|-----------------|
| The average UK cost per hire is GBP 6,125. | The average UK cost per hire is GBP 6,125 (CIPD, 2024). |
| Organisations with process-driven recruitment perform 2.6 times higher. | Organisations with process-driven recruitment perform 2.6 times higher across talent acquisition metrics (Bersin by Deloitte, 2019). |
| UK wage growth is running at 4.5% annually. | UK wage growth is running at 4.5% annually (ONS, 2025). |
| Wrong-hire costs can reach 200% of annual salary. | Wrong-hire costs can reach 200% of annual salary for leadership positions (SHRM, 2022). |

### Multiple Sources for One Claim

When a single claim draws on more than one source, separate with a semicolon:

> The three-year agency cost reaches approximately GBP 173,000 (Payscale, 2025; ONS, 2025).

### Whitepaper Cross-References

When citing an internal Exprs whitepaper alongside external data, include the whitepaper reference in parentheses after the external citation:

> Infrastructure cost per hire declines from GBP 7,200 to GBP 3,600 over 10 placements (Bersin by Deloitte, 2019; Pragmatic Costing whitepaper, Section 5).

Internal whitepaper references use the format: **(Whitepaper Name, Section X)** or **(US-CMO-XXX, Section X)**.

## 3. Bibliography / Reference List Format

Every document that contains in-text citations must include a **Bibliography** section at the end of the document.

### Entry Format

```
Author/Organisation (Year) *Title of Publication*. Publisher (if different from author). Available at: URL [Accessed: DD Month YYYY].
```

### Rules

1. **Alphabetical order** by author surname or organisation name.
2. **Italicise** the title of the publication.
3. **Include the full URL** -- not a shortened or redirect URL.
4. **Include the access date** in the format: [Accessed: 13 February 2026].
5. **One entry per source** -- do not duplicate entries for the same publication even if cited multiple times in the document.
6. **Organisation as author:** When no individual author is named, use the organisation name (e.g., CIPD, ONS, SHRM).

## 5. Document-Specific Guidelines

### Whitepapers (US-CMO-004, 005, 007)

- Full bibliography at the end of the document.
- Every section that introduces a new data point must include an in-text citation.
- Footnote-style citations (^1, ^2) are **not permitted** -- use Harvard in-text format only.

### Research Documents (US-CMO-001, 002, 003)

- Full bibliography at the end of the document.
- Citation table or research inventory should use Harvard format entries.
- Include retrieval dates on all URL sources.

### Brand Voice Guide (US-CMO-008)

- Section 10 contains an "Approved Citation Sources" quick-reference table.
- Update Section 10 whenever a new source is approved.

### Email Sequences (US-CRO-001)

- In-text citations appear naturally within email body copy: e.g., "CIPD (2024) shows..."
- Each email includes a "Data references (Harvard format)" section below the body -- this is for internal reference, not sent to the prospect.
- Email body citations should read conversationally: "Research from CIPD (2024) shows..." rather than "(CIPD, 2024)".

### Discovery Call Script (US-CRO-003)

- Data anchors within scripted questions include the source: e.g., "CIPD (2024) reports the average UK cost per hire at GBP 6,125..."
- Framework sharing sections reference both the external source and the relevant internal whitepaper section.
- Pain Signal Guide table includes a "Data Source" column.

### Objection Playbook (US-CRO-004)

- Each objection's "Data Backing" section lists full Harvard references with URLs.
- Consultative response scripts embed citations naturally: "Bersin by Deloitte (2019) research shows..."
- The Objection Pattern Analysis table includes a "Key Source" column.

### Pricing Conversation Framework (US-CRO-005)

- Data Quick-Reference Card uses full Harvard references with URLs.
- CFO-focused talking points include source citations for every numerical claim.
- Scenario walkthroughs reference specific whitepaper sections.

### Sales Onboarding Handoff Template (US-CRO-006)

- Example entries (good vs. bad) demonstrate proper citation usage.
- Section 4 instructions require Harvard-format citations for all data claims in objection documentation.
- CS team instructions reference citation requirements for customer-facing data sharing.

### Content Calendar (US-CMO-006)

- Blog post briefs include "Data source" attributions.
- Publishing cadence rules require Harvard-format citations in all published content.

## 6. Quality Check Criteria

Before any document is marked as complete, verify:

| Check | Pass Condition |
|-------|---------------|
| **Every numerical claim has a source** | No unattributed statistics, percentages, or monetary figures |
| **Every source has a direct URL** | No "see references" or "as reported by" without a URL |
| **No unattributed data** | Every data point traces to one of the 16 approved sources |
| **Bibliography at end of document** | Alphabetical, complete, no duplicates |
| **In-text citations match bibliography** | Every (Author, Year) in-text has a corresponding bibliography entry |
| **Access dates present** | All bibliography URLs include [Accessed: DD Month YYYY] |
| **No footnote-style citations** | No ^1, ^2, or [1], [2] references -- Harvard in-text only |
| **Internal whitepaper references use correct format** | (Whitepaper Name, Section X) -- no URLs required |

---

## 7. Adding New Sources

If a new external source is needed:

1. **CMO review required.** Submit the source with: full citation, URL, specific claim it supports, and why an existing approved source cannot be used.
2. **Source must be:** peer-reviewed, government-published, or from a recognised industry body (CIPD, SHRM, ONS, BLS, McKinsey, Deloitte, etc.).
3. **No proprietary Exprs data** may be cited as an external source. Internal claims use whitepaper cross-references.
4. **Once approved:** add the source to Section 4 of this document and update the Brand Voice Guide (US-CMO-008), Section 10.

## 8. Market-Specific Formatting

| Element | UK Market (Primary) | US Market (Secondary) |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **Currency** | GBP | USD |
| **Spelling** | British English (organisation, analyse, colour) | American English (organization, analyze, color) |
| **Date format** | DD Month YYYY (13 February 2026) | Month DD, YYYY (February 13, 2026) |
| **Salary references** | ONS (2025), Payscale (2025 -- UK report) | BLS (2024), Payscale (2025 -- US report) |
| **Employer costs** | HM Government (2024) -- NI contributions | BLS (2024) -- FICA and benefits |
| **Industry benchmarks** | CIPD (2024) -- UK resourcing report | SHRM (2024) -- US talent acquisition |

When creating US-market variants of existing documents, swap the market-specific sources but retain the universal sources (Bersin, McKinsey, Galorath, Crunchbase, SHRM 2022).
