# Exprs.io Brand Voice Guide

**For: CRO, Copywriter, Social Manager, SDR, AE, and all sub-agents**
**Maintained by: CMO Agent**
**Version: 1.0 | February 2026**

---

## 1. Voice Identity: Pragmatic Operational

The Exprs voice sounds like a CFO explaining an infrastructure decision to the board. It is measured, evidence-based, and understated. It treats the reader as an operational peer, not a prospect to be convinced.

### What We Sound Like

- An operations director presenting a process improvement with data
- An economist describing a structural inefficiency in clear language
- A consultant sharing industry analysis before offering a recommendation

### What We Do Not Sound Like

- A startup founder pitching at Demo Day
- A sales representative making a cold call
- A marketing agency writing ad copy

---

## 2. Five Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Description | In Practice |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| **Measured** | Careful, considered language. No superlatives. Ranges, not absolutes. | "typically reduces per-hire costs by 40-60%" not "slashes costs by up to 80%!" |
| **Evidence-based** | Every claim supported by research, data, or economic model | "Research from SHRM (2024) shows..." not "Everyone knows that..." |
| **Understated** | Let the numbers speak. British understatement preferred. | "The maths favours a different approach" not "This is incredible!" |
| **Infrastructure-framed** | Exprs is infrastructure -- a system -- not a service or product | "Install a recruitment operating system" not "Hire a better recruiter" |
| **Peer-level** | Speaking as equals. Consultative, not selling. | "Worth exploring?" not "Book a demo now!" |

## 3. Do/Don't Examples

### Email Copy

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Research from CIPD (2024) shows the average UK cost per hire is GBP 6,125. For companies making 8+ hires annually, that figure compounds significantly." | "Did you know hiring is SUPER expensive? We can help!" |
| "Worth a 15-minute conversation to compare your numbers?" | "Book a demo now before spots fill up!" |
| "The question is whether your current model scales with your growth trajectory." | "Stop wasting money on agencies!" |

### Blog Copy

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Agency fees scale linearly with salary inflation (ONS, 2025). A 5% salary increase produces a 5% higher recruitment cost with no efficiency offset." | "Agencies are ripping you off with hidden fees." |
| "The Bersin/Deloitte Talent Acquisition Maturity Model shows process-driven organisations outperform reactive ones by a factor of 2.6 (Bersin by Deloitte, 2019)." | "Our amazing platform transforms your hiring forever." |
| "Not all roles benefit from infrastructure. Some genuinely require specialist placement." | "Agencies are obsolete. Here is why." |

### Social Copy (LinkedIn)

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Five diagnostic questions before your next hire. Takes 10 minutes. Could save tens of thousands." | "THREAD: Why recruitment agencies are DEAD in 2026" |
| "Interesting CIPD data (2024): UK cost per hire averages GBP 6,125. Manager-level: GBP 19,000. What does your number look like?" | "We just launched something incredible. Check it out!" |
| "Fixed vs. variable cost structures in recruitment -- the same analysis that moved IT to cloud applies here." | "Our clients save 60% on hiring costs. DM for details!" |

### Sales Conversation

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Based on what you have described, it sounds like most of your hiring demand is systemic -- repeatable roles where infrastructure would compound efficiency." | "You should definitely switch to us -- you are overpaying." |
| "The industry data suggests companies at your stage typically spend GBP 40-60K annually on agencies (CIPD, 2024). Does that align with your experience?" | "How much are you spending? That is way too much." |
| "Some of your roles -- the VP hire, for example -- probably do benefit from a specialist agency. The question is whether all eight roles require that approach." | "Agencies cannot do what we do." |

### Discovery Call

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "I am curious about your current hiring process. How many placements did you make last year, and how did you resource them?" | "Let me tell you about our product." |
| "That is a common pattern. Most companies in the 30-60 employee range find that 70-80% of their roles are structurally repeatable." | "You are doing it wrong. Here is the right way." |
| "Would it be useful to walk through the cost comparison? I can show you what the three-year trajectory looks like with your numbers." | "Our pricing is much better. Let me show you." |

## 4. Claim-Backing Template

Every major claim in Exprs content must follow this structure:

```
"Research from [SOURCE] ([YEAR]) shows [SPECIFIC DATA POINT].
For companies with [RELEVANT CHARACTERISTIC],
this typically means [IMPLICATION].
[BRIDGE TO NEXT POINT OR CTA]."
```

**Example (Pillar A):**
"Research from Bersin by Deloitte (2019) shows that organisations with process-driven recruitment functions outperform reactive organisations by a factor of 2.6 across talent acquisition metrics. For companies making 5 or more placements per year, this suggests that investing in recruitment infrastructure -- rather than relying on transactional agency placements -- may yield significant compound efficiency gains over time."

**Example (Pillar B):**
"SHRM data (2022) indicates that wrong-hire costs range from 50 to 200 percent of annual salary. For leadership roles, where the impact extends to strategic direction and team morale, specialist placement through an experienced recruiter is often the appropriate approach. For repeatable roles -- engineers, account managers, operations staff -- infrastructure captures the learning that reduces wrong-hire probability with each cycle."

**Example (Pillar C):**
"CIPD (2024) reports the average UK cost per hire at GBP 6,125. At 20 percent agency fees on a GBP 55K median salary (Payscale, 2025), five placements cost GBP 55,000 annually. With 5 percent salary inflation (ONS, 2025), that figure reaches GBP 60,638 by year three -- an automatic increase with no efficiency gain. A fixed infrastructure model at GBP 36,000 annually is insulated from this escalation."

## 5. Cliche Avoidance List

**Never use these words or phrases in any Exprs content:**

| Category | Banned Terms |
|----------|-------------|
| Hype | game-changer, revolutionary, disruptive, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, industry-leading |
| Vague superlatives | best-in-class, world-class, state-of-the-art, next-generation, unparalleled |
| Startup jargon | synergy, leverage (as verb), circle back, touch base, deep dive (as noun), bandwidth (for capacity), move the needle |
| Marketing cliches | unlock, unleash, empower, elevate, transform (without specific evidence), reimagine |
| Recruitment hype | "the future of recruitment", "reimagining hiring", "talent revolution", "hiring reinvented" |
| Pressure language | "limited time", "act now", "do not miss out", "spots filling up", "exclusive access" |
| Competitive attacks | "better than agencies", "agencies are obsolete", "stop overpaying" (as headline/subject) |
| False urgency | "before it is too late", "your competitors are already", "falling behind" |
| Unsubstantiated claims | "saves X%" (without specifying conditions), "guaranteed results", "proven system" (without specifying proof) |
| Filler phrases | "in today's fast-paced world", "now more than ever", "it goes without saying" |

## 6. Industry Term Usage Rules

These terms are permitted and encouraged when used factually and analytically:

| Term | Correct Usage | Incorrect Usage |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Hiring** | "reducing hiring volatility through infrastructure" | "Our hiring solution transforms your team" |
| **Recruitment** | "recruitment infrastructure vs. recruitment agencies" | "Revolutionary recruitment technology" |
| **Agency** | "agency fees vs. fixed costs" | "Agencies are failing you" |
| **Candidates** | "candidate pipeline depth" | "Access to top candidates" |
| **Talent acquisition** | "talent acquisition as an operational function" | "World-class talent acquisition platform" |

The rule: use these terms matter-of-factly in analysis. Never use them with hype. Compare them analytically.

## 7. Market-Specific Tone Calibration

### UK Market
- British English: organise, colour, realise, favour, behaviour
- Understated framing: "worth a conversation" not "let's chat"
- Currency: GBP, "pounds", no dollar references in UK-targeted content
- Colloquial professionalism: "full whack", "no worries at all" in informal contexts
- Slightly wider email spacing (respect for time)

### US Market
- American English: organize, color, realize, favor, behavior
- More direct but still measured (not aggressive)
- Currency: USD, "dollars"
- Standard professional register
- Standard email spacing

## 8. Sample Copy Pieces in Brand Voice

### Sample 1: Email Subject Line + Opening (CRO use)

**Subject:** Your recruitment cost structure -- a question

**Body:**
"I have been reviewing industry data on recruitment costs for companies in the 30-80 employee range. CIPD (2024) reports the average UK cost per hire at GBP 6,125, but for companies using agencies at 20 percent of salary, the effective cost is often two to three times that figure.

I am curious whether your experience aligns with that data. If you are making 5 or more placements per year, a 15-minute conversation comparing your numbers to industry benchmarks might surface an interesting insight.

Worth exploring?"

### Sample 2: Blog Introduction (CMO/Copywriter use)

"Most operational functions improve with repetition. A customer support team that handles its hundredth ticket is more efficient than when it handled its tenth. A finance function that closes its twentieth month-end is faster and more accurate than its fifth.

Recruitment, as typically practised, does not follow this pattern. Each hire is a fresh engagement with an external provider. The screening criteria developed during the last search are not retained. The candidate pipeline generated is not owned. The process improvements identified are not implemented.

The question is not whether this is frustrating. Most founders already know it is. The question is whether it is structurally necessary."

### Sample 3: LinkedIn Post (Social Manager use)

"Interesting data from CIPD's latest Resourcing Report (2024):

Average UK cost per hire: GBP 6,125
Manager-level: GBP 19,000
Agency fees: 15-25% of first-year salary (SHRM, 2024)

For a company making 8 hires per year at blended GBP 50K median, that is GBP 80,000 in agency fees alone.

The question most founders are not asking: what does that figure look like in year three, after salary inflation has compounded (ONS, 2025)?

More on this in our Pragmatic Costing analysis [link]."

### Sample 4: Objection Response (CRO/AE use)

**Prospect says:** "We already have an internal recruiter."

**Response:** "That makes sense. An internal recruiter is a person -- someone who understands your culture, your team dynamics, and what a good hire looks like for your organisation. What we are describing is the infrastructure that person operates within. Job description templates that improve with each use. Screening rubrics that calibrate over time. A candidate pipeline that accumulates rather than resetting. It is the system that amplifies what your recruiter can do, not a replacement for them."

### Sample 5: Pricing Introduction (CRO/AE use)

"Before we discuss pricing, it might be useful to frame the comparison. Your current model is variable -- percentage-based fees that scale with salary and volume. What we are proposing is a fixed operational expense: GBP 3,000 per month, regardless of how many placements you make or how salary markets move.

The question is not which is cheaper in any given month. The question is what the three-year cost trajectory looks like under each model. If you are comfortable, I can walk through the numbers using your hiring volume and typical salary ranges."

## 9. Voice Review Checklist

Before publishing any content or launching any sequence, verify:

- [ ] Every major claim backed by a specific, cited source in Harvard format -- e.g. (SHRM, 2024)
- [ ] Zero instances of banned cliches (Section 5)
- [ ] Industry terms used factually, not for hype (Section 6)
- [ ] Tone reads as CFO/operations director, not marketing agency
- [ ] Content maps to one or more of the three pillars
- [ ] No competitive attacks ("better than X", "agencies fail at Y")
- [ ] Consultative framing maintained (diagnosis, not prescription)
- [ ] Appropriate market calibration (UK vs. US English, currency, register)
- [ ] CTAs are peer-level ("worth exploring?" not "book now!")
- [ ] Numbers include methodology or source, not just assertions
- [ ] In-text citations match entries in bibliography/reference list
