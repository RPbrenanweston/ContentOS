## Template Purpose

Every closed deal must be documented using this template. The handoff serves three functions:

1. **Customer Success onboarding:** CSM understands the buyer's pain, expectations, and agreed milestones
2. **CRO analytics:** Which pillars close deals, which objections recur, and where pricing friction exists
3. **CMO feedback loop:** What messaging resonated, what did not, and where positioning needs updating

**Rule:** Complete this template within 24 hours of deal close. Incomplete handoffs delay onboarding and lose context.

## Examples of Good vs. Bad Handoff Entries

### Good Pain Point Documentation

> "The founder expressed frustration that each engineering hire costs approximately GBP 13,000 through their agency (above the CIPD (2024) average of GBP 6,125), and the process 'starts from scratch every time' -- a knowledge retention issue per SHRM (2022). They have hired four engineers in the past 12 months and plan six more in the next year. The specific trigger was receiving a GBP 14,500 invoice for a senior hire and realising the total annual agency spend was GBP 52,000."

### Bad Pain Point Documentation

> "Customer does not like agencies."

### Good Objection Documentation

> "The CFO asked why they should commit to GBP 36K/year when their agency only charges per placement. I walked through the 3-year TCO comparison (Pragmatic Costing whitepaper, Section 3) using their volume (6 hires/year at GBP 50K median (Payscale, 2025), 20% fee (SHRM, 2024)). The agency trajectory was GBP 189K vs. GBP 108K infrastructure. The CFO's concern shifted from absolute cost to cost trajectory. Resolved."

### Bad Objection Documentation

> "Price concern. Handled."

### Good Milestone Documentation

> "Month 1: Have three engineering role briefs ready using infrastructure methodology. Month 2: First two engineers hired through infrastructure with time-to-fill under 30 days (McKinsey (2024) indicates 15% timeline reduction with structured approaches). Month 3: Cost per hire for engineering roles below GBP 5,000 (per CPH decline curve, Pragmatic Costing whitepaper, Section 5)."

### Bad Milestone Documentation

> "Month 3: Everything working."

## Instructions for Customer Success Team

1. **Read this handoff document before the first customer interaction.** Understand their pain, their expectations, and the milestones they agreed to.

2. **Reference the pillar that closed the deal** in onboarding messaging. If Pillar A (Steady-State) resonated, frame onboarding around compound efficiency (Bersin by Deloitte, 2019). If Pillar C (Pragmatic Costing) resonated, frame around cost tracking and TCO improvement (Galorath, 2020).

3. **Monitor the 90-day milestones.** Each milestone is a check-in point. If a milestone is at risk, escalate early.

4. **Use the customer's own success definition** (Section 6) in your 30/60/90-day reviews. They defined success -- measure against their definition, not ours.

5. **Feed back to CRO.** If the customer raises new objections during onboarding, document them and send through OBJECTION-NEW gate. If pricing friction emerges post-close, send through PRICING-FRICTION gate.

6. **All customer-facing data claims must use Harvard-format citations** per CITATION-STANDARD.md. Reference specific whitepaper sections and external sources when sharing benchmarks or projections.
