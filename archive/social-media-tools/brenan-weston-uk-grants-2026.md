## TIER 1: Apply Now (Open or Imminent)

### 1. R&D Tax Credits (RDEC Scheme) ⚡ START IMMEDIATELY

**Funding**: 20% credit rate = ~15% net benefit on qualifying R&D spend
**Type**: Retrospective cash credit via HMRC
**Match Funding**: N/A (claimed after expenditure)
**Application**: Annual tax return via specialist advisor

**Brenan Weston Eligibility**: ⚠️ **CONDITIONAL** (requires identifiable R&D activity)

**Critical Distinction for Consultancies**:

**What QUALIFIES**:
- ✅ Developing proprietary AI tools for security talent matching
- ✅ Building custom AI integrations for cyber recruitment workflows
- ✅ Creating novel data pipelines or algorithms for security candidate assessment
- ✅ Research into AI-powered threat intelligence correlation for talent forecasting
- ✅ Training specialized AI models for cybersecurity domain expertise

**What DOES NOT Qualify**:
- ❌ Using existing AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) for client delivery without novel development
- ❌ Standard consultancy work applying existing knowledge to clients
- ❌ Routine candidate sourcing using off-the-shelf platforms
- ❌ Normal business operations and client relationship management

**The Bright Line Test**: Are you building new AI technology, or using existing AI tools? Only the former qualifies. **HMRC is increasingly skeptical of consultancy R&D claims** — documentation must prove technological advancement beyond standard practice.

**Application Complexity**: HIGH (requires specialist R&D tax advisor with consultancy experience)

**⚡ ACTION**: Engage specialist (GrantTree, Alexander Clifford, or equivalent) for assessment. **If you are building proprietary AI tools (not just using ChatGPT/Claude), document everything NOW**. If not, R&D credits may not be available.

## Key Insights

### The Consultancy Challenge
The consultancy business model significantly limits eligibility for standard innovation grants (Smart Grants, Innovation Loans, Growth Catalyst), which are designed for product/technology businesses. **Unless Brenan Weston is building a distinct product/platform/SaaS offering**, these routes are mostly closed.

### The Cybersecurity Advantage
**BUT** — cybersecurity has dedicated funding streams that consultancies CAN access:
- Cyber Local (100% funded, £25K-£150K)
- DASA/UKDI (£100K-£350K, reopening July 2026)
- AI Assurance Innovation Fund (£11M, Spring 2026)

These programmes value **domain expertise and cyber ecosystem contribution**, not just product development. Consultancies with cybersecurity focus have a structural advantage here.

### The Product Pivot Option
If Brenan Weston wants access to the broader innovation grant landscape (Smart Grants, Innovation Loans, etc.), the business model must evolve toward **product + consultancy hybrid**. Examples:
- Build SaaS platform for cyber recruitment (product) + consultancy services (service)
- Develop AI security talent marketplace (product) + advisory services (service)
- Create cyber skills gap analysis tool (product) + recruitment consultancy (service)

This "product anchor" unlocks £100K-£5M+ funding routes currently inaccessible.

### The AI Assurance Opportunity
The AI Assurance Innovation Fund (Spring 2026, £11M) is **the single most strategic opportunity** for Brenan Weston. It positions the business not as "another recruitment consultancy" but as a **thought leader in AI security assurance** — a differentiator that compounds in value. **This alone justifies the effort**.

### The Compound Effect
- Small grant (Cyber Local £25K-£150K) → builds Innovate UK track record
- R&D tax credits → funds tool development
- AI Assurance Fund win → positions as AI security thought leader
- DASA funding (July 2026) → national security credibility
- Each win opens doors to government/NCSC contracts beyond grants
